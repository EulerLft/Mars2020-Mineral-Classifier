The Delta Front Campaign began in April 2022 (Sol 400), marking a transition from the igneous crater floor to the ancient sedimentary fan deposits of Jezero Crater. This phase of the mission is dedicated to exploring layered rocks deposited by a river system billions of years ago.

### Geological Context
The Delta Front is characterized by sedimentary sequences that provide a record of Mars' aqueous history. Unlike the volcanic units of the crater floor, these formations were shaped by water-driven transport and deposition.
- *Shenandoah* Formation: Represents the lower delta units. It is a primary target for seeking potential biosignatures trapped in ancient silt.
- *Wildcat Ridge* Formation: A prominent outcrop within the delta known.
- *Skinner Ridge* Formation: An area featuring well-defined sedimentary layering, providing insights into the varying energy levels of the ancient river flow.

### Campaign Info
- Duration: ~700+ Sols (ongoing since Sol 400).
- Distance Traversed: ~15+ km total distance reached within the delta.
- Primary Minerals: Alteration/High Alteation.
- Key Targets: Dourbes, Quartier, Bellegarde.
